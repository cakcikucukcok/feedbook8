Daniel Deleanu's Principles of Logosophy, written in "logosophistic English"-a language based on the author's original concept of logoarchetype-breaks all the boundaries of human knowledge through a reinterpretation of the Platonic concept of archetype and of the Logos. Deleanu links C.G.Jung's notion of archetype with that of the Logos, as it appears in Plato, the Stoics, Philo of Alexandria and the Gospel according to John.

Making use of an original type of English, whose principal scope is to liberate language and utilize it to express the deeper realities of human knowledge, Daniel Deleanu's new scholarly system, named "logosophism", proves that the world is totally interconnected through the ontic-ontologic dimensions of its main archetype, the Word.
