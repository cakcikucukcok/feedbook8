Siddharths poems, written in his early teens, delve into a variety of issues, analysed from a schoolboys perspective, and range from the problems of farmers and racial discrimination to the grandeur of nature. They also create picturesque scenes, where one can be transported to a world of wildlife, surrounded by the beauty of nature and the joys of life. Some of his poems are descriptions of lives of people, leaving the message of hope, of light in darkness and of victory in the face of overwhelming odds. Siddharths poems bring out, as he puts it, the world in my mind.
