Since his debut bestseller, August 9th, the unforgettable memoir of love infused with a Rock n' Roll beat and the backdrop of the tech revolution, reviewers have praised Stu Schreiber's unique storytelling. Not limited by one genre, his second novel, January Chronicles: Isaac's Legacy, is a suspense thriller featuring a strong female protagonist chosen to execute the most important mission in history.

Imagine:

It's the summer of 1963, and  a 17 year old child prodigy becomes the youngest person ever hired by the NSA.

Fifty years later, this genius retires, having built much of the NSA's technology, hearing more noise and signals from earth and beyond than anyone in history and also predicting the two most significant events of his lifetime.

It's a beautiful September morning in 2017, and a stunning single Mom, January Cooper, her precocious  eleven year old daughter, Isabella, and their white lab take their customary walk to the park.

Screams of MOMMY, MOMMY reverberate through the park as Isabella discovers the lifeless body of their frail neighbor hunched over on a park bench.

A quiet life in the small town of Raleigh, IL, is turned upside down for Jan Cooper, the sole reporter for the local newspaper, when cryptic clues left by her neighbor confront her with a near-impossible puzzle. With the help of her boss at the Star-News and her on-again, off-again NSA spy boyfriend, Jan is led on an ominous journey that will determine the fate of our world.
